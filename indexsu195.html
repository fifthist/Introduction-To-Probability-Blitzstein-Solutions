a. F(x) is continuous at its given endpoints: F(1)=2πarcsin(1)=2ππ2=1 and F(0)=0, and F(x) is differentiable between 0 and 1:

b. F'(x)=f(x)=2πddx(arcsin(√x))=2π(1√1−x)(1√x)

This is a valid PDF despite the discontinuities at 0 and 1 as the integral of f(x) from 0 to 1 converges, and f(x) is always positive - the same reason why 1√x has a discontinuity at x=0 but can be integrated from 0 to any positive real number.
