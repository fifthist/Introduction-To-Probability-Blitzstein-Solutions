∫∞−∞e−x2∕2dx=√2π

. Since the integrand is an even function, the integral from

−∞

to

0

Φ(z)=12+1√2π∫z0e−x2∕2dx

By applying the variable substitution u=x∕√2 in the integral, we obtain du=dx∕√2. The lower and upper limits of integration become 0 and z∕√2, respectively.

Φ(z)=12+1√2π∫z∕√20e−u2√2du=12+122√π∫z∕√20e−u2du
