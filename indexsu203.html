5.2.7 problem 59

(a) Let θ1, θ2 and θ3 be the angles corresponding to points A, B and C respectively. From the statement of the problem, those angles are i.i.d. Unif(0,2π).

The angles θ1, θ2 and θ3 divide the [0,2π) range in four successive sub-intervals. The argument is wrong because the problem is not symmetric with respect to the three arcs, but rather with respect to the four angle sub-intervals. The average length of each sub-interval is 2π∕4=π∕2, by symmetry. The length of the arc that contains the point (1,0) is the sum of the first and fourth sub-intervals, so it is twice as long as the other arcs on average.
