6.2.1 problem 15

(a). Since the MGF is uniquely determined from the distribution, and X and Y are identically distributed,

MY2(t)=MX2(t)=(1−2t)−12.

As X and Y are mutually independent,

MW(t)=MX2+Y2(t)=MX2(t)⋅MY2(t).

Hence,

MW(t)=1(1−2t).

(b). Exponential distribution, with λ=12. Note that this is a special case (as mentioned in the question) of the Gamma distribution (Chapter 8).
