4.5.2 problem 71

Let Ij be the indicator random variable for pair j having the aforementioned property. P(Ij=1)=13652, under the assumption that the probability of being born on a particular day is 1365. Note that since we don’t know anything about the age of the kids, we are assuming their mothers are also equally likely to be born on any of the 365 days.

Then, the expected number of pairs with the aforementioned property is E(X)=(902)13652≈0.03.

Let Z∼Poiss(0.03) model the distribution of pairs with the desired property. Then, probability that there is at least one such pair is 1−P(Z=0)=1−e−0.03≈1−(1−0.03)=0.03=3100.
