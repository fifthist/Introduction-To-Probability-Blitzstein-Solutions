5.3.3 problem 43

a. The first success occurs at times kΔt, where k≥0 is the number of failures before the first success. Therefore, T=GΔt.

b. Since the trials are independent and with the same probability of success λΔt, the number of failures before the first success is G∼Geom(λΔt) by the story of the geometric. The PMF of G is given by

P(G=k)=(1−λΔt)kλΔt , for k=0,1,…

It is convenient to calculate P(G>k)

P(G>k)=∞∑i=k+1P(G=i)=λΔt∞∑i=k+1(1−λΔt)i=(1−λΔt)k+1

The CDF of T is calculated as

P(T≤t)=1−P(T>t)=1−P(G>t∕Δt)=1−(1−λΔt)tΔt+1

c. Taking the limit of the CDF of T for Δt→0

limΔt→0P(T≤t)=limΔt→0[1−(1−λΔt)tΔt+1]=1−limΔt→0(1−λΔt)tΔt+1

If we do the substitution n=t∕Δt, we obtain n→∞ for Δt→0

limΔt→0P(T≤t)=1−limn→∞(1−λtn)n+1=1−limn→∞(1−λtn)n×limn→∞(1−λtn)

The first limit is the compount interest limit and is equal to e−λt. The second limit is equal to 1. Thus,

limΔt→0P(T≤t)=1−e−λt

which is the CDF of the Expo(λ) distribution. Therefore, T converges to Expo(λ) as trials are performed faster and with smaller success probabilities.
