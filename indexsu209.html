5.3.4 problem 47

a. Let’s develop the integral ∫t0h(s)ds

∫t0h(s)ds=∫t0f(s)1−F(s)ds=∫t0f(s)G(s)ds

where G(t)=1−F(t) is the survival function.

Doing the substitution v=logG(s), we have

dv=G'(s)G(s)ds=−F'(s)G(s)ds=−f(s)G(s)ds

With the substitution, the lower limit of integration becomes logG(0)=log1=0, and the upper limit becomes logG(t). Therefore,

∫t0h(s)ds=∫logG(t)0−dv=−logG(t)		(5.36)

logG(t)=−∫t0h(s)ds

G(t)=1−F(t)=exp(−∫t0h(s)ds)

F(t)=1−exp(−∫t0h(s)ds) , for all t>0


b. The PDF is the derivative of the CDF

f(t)=F'(t)=[1−exp(−∫t0h(s)ds)]'

f(t)=−exp(−∫t0h(s)ds)⋅(−∫t0h(s)ds)'		(5.37)

To calculate the derivative of the integral in the relation above, let’s apply the differential in both sides of equation (5.36)

(∫t0h(s)ds)'=(−logG(t))'=−G'(t)G(t)=(F(t)−1)'G(t)=f(t)1−F(t)=h(t)

Substituting this result in equation (5.37)

f(t)=−exp(−∫t0h(s)ds)⋅(−h(t))

f(t)=h(t)exp(−∫t0h(s)ds) , for all t>0
